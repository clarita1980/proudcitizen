In-home Observation for Capia

March 2009 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Etnographic Researcher

Project definition, goals and objectives

Nielsen was hired by Capia to understand why, how and when argentine women consume eggs.

The goal of the research was to explore and discover how consumers use and represent eggs in their diet, identifying styles of usage, habits and ways of purchasing.


Process: methods and techniques

We requested the client information about past studies and performed a preliminary desk research to contextualize the upcoming one. We performed four observations and in-depth ethnographic interviews with women in their Buenos Aires homes while cooking with eggs. We spent a full day with each woman having a conversation about the role of the woman in nowadays society, the role of the woman as a mother, the role of the diet and specifically the use and representation of eggs in their cooking habits.

Highlights:

  • Brand is not a key element in the egg purchase decision process.
  • Eggs have protein similar to meat but consumers don't think or care about the different types of eggs providing different amounts of protein as they do when buying meat.
  • The interviewed women did not signify eggs as a priority in their diets despite the fact that eggs are used as a cooking ingredient in a majority of their meals.
  • Farm eggs create an ambivalent sensation: they are more natural and fresh but the presence of a feather or other imperfection produces disgust.
  • Consumers are not sure if eggs contain cholesterol or not.
  • There is not a clear value proposition around the egg.
