Nature Valley Granola Shopper

June 2010 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Etnographic Researcher

Project definition, goals and objectives

Nature Valley wanted to perform an exploratory research about the cereal bars category. The goal of the research was to understand the behavior of cereal bar buyers in-store and priotize the drivers that influence the final decision. Also gain qualitative information about the cereal bars consumer to build personas and empathize with their needs and desires.


Process: methods and techniques

We requested the client information about past research and performed a preliminary desk research to contextualize the study. This research was performed on team with two other anthropologists. We interviewed in-store men and women buyers of the brand as well as from the competitors brands. We video recorded the buying process and once the purchase decision was made we showed the video to the interviewee to go step-by-step understanding and analysing the influencers in the decision.

Highlights:

  • Cereal bars are perceived as a substitute snack for cookies and chocolates between lunch and dinner with a bit more nutrition or less fat.
  • Consumers do not put cereal bars on their supermarket shopping list.
  • Although they are available in large supermarkets, consumers tend to buy them in kiosks on the street.
  • There are opportunities for point-of-purchase events to influence the purchase.
  • Cereal bars are consumed on-the-go and mostly with soda.
  • One important purchase driver is the texture of the bar: soft and humid vs. dry and hard.
  • With new ingredients like chocolate or yogurt the cereal bars are seen as the healthy version of candies.
