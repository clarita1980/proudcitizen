Project definition, goals and objectives

The goal of the research was to understand the emotional, rational and sensorial aspects linked to the deodorant in different situations / moments (going out / seduction, hygiene, aesthetics), the relationship with the five senses and the others, as well as recommendations for improvements on the communicational approach of the brand.


Process: methods and techniques

We requested the client information about past research and performed a preliminary desk research to contextualize the study.We performed 12 days in the life of different users. With this technique we focused on sharing time and space with the person accompanying him/ her during the activities and interacting while observing and recording.

Highlights:

  • Users might not take a shower everyday but the application of the deos is daily, even without a previous shower.
  • A smelly armpit (as well as smelly hair) represents a dirty person as a whole.
  • We can build two models around time chosen for washing themselves: people who bathe early in the morning to start the day clean and fresh and people who bathe at the end of the day to go to bed clean and remove the dirt from the day. Each of these models has an impact on a general routine that each model will develop throughout the day.
  • For most interviees DEOS appears linked to cover odors and as an obligation versus perfume that is linked to pleasant smell and seduction.
  • Is the context that punish or not the odor. In the context of a physical activity like in the gym it is legitime but during a regular meeting the same smell it is punished.
