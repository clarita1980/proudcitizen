La Salteña Shopper

August 2010 - Buenos Aires, Argentina - Research commissioned by Nielsen.

Etnographic Researcher

Project definition, goals and objectives

Nielsen hired us to develop a shopper study for La Salteña. The goal of the research was to identify key drivers when purchasing pastas and dough and insights for improving the brand image and communication in-store.


Process: methods and techniques

We requested the client information about past research and performed a preliminary desk research to contextualize the study.This research was performed on team with two other anthropologists. We observed men and women buying pasta or dough of the brand (what they do) as well as from the competitors in supermarkets and mini-markets as well as casual interviews to recuperate their thoughts behind the decision (what they say) .

Highlights:

  • Shoppers compared pastas to foods such as hamburgers and hot dogs.
  • Ingredients like meat and chicken do not have a good reputation inside the pastas since it is associated to processed meat.
  • Shoppers identified a gap in the market offering referring to wheat dough and pastas made with seeds.
  • There are some difficulties with La Salteña pasta packaging. Users have har time finding and recognizing the ingredients.
  • When out of the pocket moments, users tend to buy one package of a premium brand like La Salteña and and the other from a cheaper brand.
