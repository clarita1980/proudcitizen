The Interview Project

Building community through storytelling




“I like to see a man proud of the place in which he lives. I like to see a man live so that his place will be proud of him.”
― Abraham Lincoln

Why ask citizens about pride?
  1. Every year in the month of June, San Francisco celebrates it's world famous Pride Celebration & Parade. I was wondering if there is reciprocal causation between pride and civic engagement in other spheres.
  2. It seems that pride is a bit undervalued by citizens (except certain situations/groups as mentioned above) and overrated by governments. I wanted to understand how ordinary citizens react to the question about pride in their local cities.
  3. As a new citizen to the bay area, I wanted to collect stories about what's important to citizens and what creates a unique sense of place.
  4. I am passionate about ordinary and extraordinary stories. Stories connect to our emotions and rational. Stories inspire new behaviors. The desire to discover, explore, understand and promote cross - cultural stories and register those experiences is a growing part of my life. Community storytelling draws in more voices, creates connections, builds empathy by providing a human face to the complex challenges facing communities nowadays. Story is action. Through listening to other;s stories, we participate in the community.
  5. The benefits of storytelling are immense: increased engagement with community members; increased visibility; exposure and connection with causes.
