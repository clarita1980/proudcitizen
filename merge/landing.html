Clara Gonzalez Sueyro

User Experience and Ethnographic Research Specialist




Hello my name is Clara and I am an anthropologist and researcher from Buenos Aires, Argentina. I have been researching for corporate, non-profit and startups clients using ethnographic research to understand fundamental user behavior through observation and in depth interviews, amongst other techniques. The ethnographic research allows the identification of behaviors, needs and tensions in the same context of use or consumption and it seems an obvious statement but it is not.

I leverage my academic background in qualitative research, my years of consumer market research and my startup to improve user experience and business outcomes. My experience tackles the design of the proposal, the moderation (usability, in-depth interviews, focus groups), the collection and analysis of results, as well as presentation and implementation of findings if required.

Specialities:

  • Contextual inquiry and observation
  • Ethnographic research: intimate knowledge of users needs and desires
  • Consumer Research and Shopping trips
  • In-depth interviews, video recording and summarizing
  • Persona development and documentation
  • Wireframing and prototyping
  • User testing and usability studies
  • Creative Sessions

Clients:

I have been performing diverse studies for companies such as Unilever, Beiersdorf-Nivea, ESPN, Kraft, Molinos Rio de la Plata, Syngenta, Sodexho, Banco Galicia, Alicorp, ICSC, General Mills, Clorox, Carrefour, amongst others. I have worked in partnership with market research consulting and innovation agencies such as Nielsen, Ipsos, BMC, Navarro and Neelus.

My 10 research commitments:

  • Watch people doing stuff
  • Listen what they say, listen to the meaning behind each word
  • Pay attention to body language and gestures
  • Put everything in context
  • Pay attention to details
  • Don't guess
  • Explicit your assumptions
  • Don't prove, just understand
  • Investigate the inconsistence between what they say and what they do
  • Don't sell anything!
