Project definition, goals and objectives

The non profit Doncel hired me and a colleague to design, coordinate and implement a project awarded by The Youth Innovation Fund (YlF). The project consisted of training workshops for youth facilitators to develop the main content to incorporate in a web page for care leavers, as well as acquire the basic pedagogical methods necessary to share their experience and communicate it successfully to youth currently in care, using the peer to peer methodology.

The goal was that trained youths learnt how to give a positive, and at the same time realistic approach, about preparation and experiences they had to youth still in care by producing a toolkit website used by all participants.


Process: methods and techniques

  • Focus groups and participatory meetings to collect the most relevant information from the youth’s points of views.
  • Workshops to translate, together with the youths, the results of the focus groups into categories, systematizing information to build up texts in appropriate language that made it ready to be used in the webpage
  • Develop the webpage content.
  • Creative Session
  • In-depth interviews to youth
  • Video interviews to youth
  • Workshops to train 10 facilitators, chosen amongst participants in the Focus Groups.

Highlights:
